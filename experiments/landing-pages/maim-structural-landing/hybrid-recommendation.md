# Hybrid Recommendation — Portal + Structural Page

## Recommendation

Use both concepts, but assign them different jobs.

The Gravity Well is the opening portal.

The NEXT-style structure is the practical conversion page.

Together they become the first version of MAIM Mission Control.

## Page Architecture

### Option 1 — Safe Launch

Use the structural page only.

Best for:

- Fast launch
- Mobile safety
- Simple editing
- Clear webinar registration

### Option 2 — Premium Launch

Use Gravity Well hero at the top, then structural page underneath.

Best for:

- Strong brand impression
- Universe / movement feeling
- Premium homepage experience

### Option 3 — Split Funnel

Use two pages:

- `/` = structural landing page
- `/mission-control` = Gravity Well universe experience

Best for:

- Testing without risking conversion
- Letting the portal become a deeper brand experience later

## Current Best Move

Ship structural first if speed matters.

Test Gravity Well separately.

Merge only after performance and clarity review.

## Why

The practical page answers the visitor's questions quickly.

The universe page creates emotion.

Emotion without clarity loses signups.

Clarity without emotion can feel generic.

MAIM needs both.

## Suggested Final Homepage Flow

1. Gravity Well universe hero
2. Sticky simple nav
3. What is MAIM?
4. Facts at a glance
5. The Translator
6. ABC Roadmap
7. Live Knowledge Sessions
8. Discovery Call / Register CTA
9. FAQ
10. Footer

## Design Rule

Keep the luxury editorial rhythm from the structural page.

Keep the universe motion from Gravity Well only where it adds meaning.

No motion for motion's sake.

No complexity for complexity's sake.

The page must still feel like Major speaking directly to someone.
