# Decision Notes — MAIM Structural Landing Experiment

## Current Decision

Proceed with a second landing page experiment.

This is the practical page candidate.

The Gravity Well is the emotional portal candidate.

## Read on the NEXT Layout

The layout is strong because it is simple, premium, and structured.

It uses:

- A clean sticky nav
- Dark background
- Gold accents
- Editorial serif headings
- Large spacious sections
- Direct CTAs
- Facts at a glance
- Proof blocks
- FAQ

That structure is better for conversion than a fully immersive 3D page.

## What We Should Borrow

Borrow:

- Section rhythm
- Editorial typography
- Facts-at-a-glance row
- Founder/architect section
- Methodology cards
- Day/session breakdown
- FAQ accordion
- Final CTA

Do not borrow:

- The exact offer
- The exact price framing
- The exact cohort scarcity language
- The Phil-specific architect copy
- The full 24-section length for first public MAIM launch

## MAIM Translation

The NEXT says:

Stop carrying your life's work in your head. Build it.

MAIM should say:

Stop carrying your ideas in your head. Build them with AI.

The NEXT positions an architect.

MAIM positions a translator.

The NEXT sells a founder cohort.

MAIM starts with free knowledge sessions and grows into labs, cohorts, discovery calls, and implementation.

## Hybrid Recommendation

Create a two-layer landing system:

### Layer 1: Portal Hero

Use the Gravity Well hero if performance passes.

Purpose:

- Emotion
- Universe
- Brand memory
- Differentiation

### Layer 2: Structural Page

Use the NEXT-style structure below the hero.

Purpose:

- Explain
- Build trust
- Show the path
- Capture signups

## If Only One Can Ship First

Ship the structural version first.

It is safer, faster, clearer, and easier to edit.

Use Gravity Well as a premium upgrade after testing.

## Best Use Case

For this week:

Use structural version as the quick public landing page.

For the full MAIM Mission Control:

Use Gravity Well as the top portal and this structural page as the body.

## Final Rule

The page must answer three questions within five seconds:

1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. What do I do next?

If the page cannot answer those, simplify.
