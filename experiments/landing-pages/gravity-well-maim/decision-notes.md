# Decision Notes — Gravity Well MAIM Landing Test

## Current Decision

Proceed with a controlled experiment.

Do not publish this directly as the MAIM homepage.

Do not merge into main until the test passes the UI, UX, performance, and brand review.

## Why This Template Is Interesting

The Gravity Well opening creates the feeling of entering a universe.

That is strategically aligned with MAIM because Major AI Mindset is not positioned as a normal AI course.

It is a mindset operating system.

People are not only learning tools.

They are being invited into a new way of seeing problems, possibilities, systems, and legacy.

The template communicates that emotionally before the copy explains it rationally.

## Risk

The original template is visually strong but conversion-risky.

Risks:

- Too abstract for cold visitors
- Heavy WebGL load on mobile
- Possible distraction from signup CTA
- Technical feel may overpower the human-centered MAIM message
- Original Superset identity is not MAIM-specific
- Too much motion may hurt accessibility

## Correct Use

Use the Gravity Well as the first-screen portal only.

Then transition into grounded MAIM sections:

- What MAIM is
- Who it is for
- The next live knowledge session
- The ABC roadmap
- The founder story
- Signup form

## Pro Max UI/UX Rule

Wonder first.

Clarity second.

Action always visible.

The hero may inspire, but it must not hide the next step.

## Hero Copy Test

Primary:

Major AI Mindset

The Mindset Operating System for the AI Age

See it. Believe it. Build it.

Reserve your seat for the next live knowledge session.

CTA:

Reserve Your Seat

Secondary CTA:

Explore the ABC Roadmap

## What Must Change From The Original Template

Replace:

Superset

With:

Major AI Mindset

Replace mode labels:

Stack / Orbit

With:

Roadmap / Universe

Replace coordinate readout or make it optional.

Use sphere labels or tooltips connected to the ABCs.

Use MAIM colors instead of raw neon-only palette.

Keep the gravity well feeling, but remove anything that feels unrelated to MAIM.

## Test Criteria

This experiment can move forward only if:

1. The hero loads quickly on mobile.
2. The CTA is visible above the fold.
3. The copy makes sense without scrolling.
4. Reduced-motion fallback works.
5. The 3D background does not block form use.
6. The page still feels human, not just technical.
7. The design aligns with MAIM Brand System Vol. 00.

## Recommendation

Build both versions.

Version A gives us the safe launch page.

Version B gives us the cinematic portal.

If Version B slows the page down or confuses people, keep it as a later premium homepage hero, not the first signup page.

If Version B performs well, promote it into the public landing page.
