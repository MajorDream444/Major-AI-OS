# MAIM Command Room Landing Page Design Review

Status: Design direction review
Owner: Major Dream Williams
Date: 2026-07-05

## Verdict

Use the Google AI Studio direction as the design source.

Do not use the current Codex interpretation as the visual source.

Codex should not redesign this from scratch.

Codex should implement the approved Studio direction with restraint.

## What Major Approved

The preferred direction is the AI Studio landing page feel:

- Command room
- Live knowledge room
- Signal deck
- Schedule for the week
- Next knowledge session
- Google AI Studio one-shot energy
- Premium black interface
- Gold, green, blue, and orange accents
- Technical enough to feel like a system
- Human enough to not feel like a generic SaaS page

## What Needs Correction

The Codex pass moved too close to the social media ad and flyer language.

It became too loud in places.

It felt like a poster stretched into a landing page instead of a true landing page experience.

The landing page should not feel like:

- A flyer
- A social media graphic
- A webinar ad
- A generic AI course page
- A sales page with too much shouting

The page should feel like:

- A room
- A command view
- A live learning environment
- A calm premium system
- A doorway into MAIM
- A schedule and onboarding hub

## Keep From AI Studio Direction

Keep:

- The dark grid command room background
- The top navigation with MAIM logo and Live Knowledge Room label
- The tri-color top signal line
- The command-view panel on the hero
- The right-side See It / Believe It / Build It cards
- The weekly rhythm and next session framing
- The feeling that the visitor is entering a system
- The restrained interface language

## Refine

Refine:

- Logo usage should be accurate and clean
- Colors should follow the locked MAIM palette
- The orange should be used as action energy, not as dominant flyer energy
- Typography should remain strong but not over-shout every section
- The schedule should be visible and useful
- The register section should feel like a system input, not a flyer block
- The page should answer what is this, who is it for, and what do I do next within five seconds

## UI/UX Pro Max Review

Yes, use the Pro Max UI/UX lens.

Criteria:

- Can a visitor understand the page within five seconds?
- Does it feel like a landing page, not a flyer?
- Is the next session visible?
- Is the CTA clear?
- Is the brand premium but still human?
- Does it preserve the AI Studio command-room feel?
- Does it avoid generic AI-course language?
- Does the mobile layout still feel intentional?
