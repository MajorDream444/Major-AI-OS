# MAIM Weekly Knowledge Session Video Plan

## Purpose

This document updates the ABC video direction for the weekly Major AI Mindset knowledge sessions.

The videos are no longer just short alphabet posts.

They are weekly teaching assets that introduce the mindset required to build with AI, starting with the ABCs:

- Awareness
- Belief
- Context

The goal is to prepare beginners emotionally, mentally, and practically before they touch tools, agents, automations, Claude Code, Codex, Remotion, GitHub, or advanced workflows.

Major AI Mindset is not an AI course first.

It is a mindset operating system for the AI age.

## Weekly Video Positioning

These videos should feel like invitations into a live learning rhythm.

They should say:

- You are not behind.
- You do not need to be technical to begin.
- The work starts with how you think.
- The ABCs are the first operating system.
- AI becomes useful when your awareness, belief, and context are clear.

The weekly knowledge session should feel like a room people can enter, not a product being pushed at them.

## Audience

The primary viewer is not a tech insider.

They are:

- A business owner
- A creator
- A parent
- A coach
- A church leader
- A wellness practitioner
- An elder
- A student
- A retired athlete
- A community builder
- Someone with experience but no leverage
- Someone who is curious about AI but overwhelmed by the language around it

The videos should lower fear and raise readiness.

## Core Message

The first weekly knowledge session is about the mindset needed to begin.

The spine:

> Before you learn the tools, you need the mindset.
>
> The ABCs are where we start.
>
> Awareness shows you what is happening.
> Belief helps you enter the conversation.
> Context teaches AI how to help you.
>
> Likkle by likkle, we build the future.

Title:

`This Is Not Just About AI`

Invite people into the weekly knowledge session and make the ABCs feel necessary, not childish.

Core idea:

AI is moving fast, but the first step is not technical. The first step is mindset.

```text
AI is moving fast.
But the first step is not learning every tool.
The first step is learning how to think in this new age.
That is why we are starting with the ABCs.
Awareness.
Belief.
Context.
Before the agents, automations, and apps, we build the mindset.
Likkle by likkle, we build the future.
```

Visual direction:

Major standing in a calm black-and-gold editorial space. The MAIM seal floats subtly above or behind him. The letters A, B, and C appear one at a time in gold, green, and champion blue. The tone should feel like a weekly opening ritual.

`Join this week's Major AI Mindset knowledge session.`

### Video 3 — A Is For Awareness

Title:

`Notice Before You Automate`

Purpose:

Teach the first mindset lesson for the weekly session.

Core idea:

You cannot automate what you have not noticed.

Short script:

```text
A is for Awareness.
Most people want AI to save them time.
But first, you have to notice where your time is leaking.
What repeats every week?
What drains your energy?
What keeps getting delayed?
Awareness comes before automation.
Start there.
```

Visual direction:

Use the established A Awareness footage and stills: invisible backpacks, calendars, emails, notifications, repeated tasks, one glowing opportunity or repeated task becoming visible.

CTA:

`Comment AWARENESS if you found one task AI could help with.`

### Video 4 — B Is For Belief

Title:

`If You Can Text, You Can Use AI`

Purpose:

Lower fear and help beginners feel capable.

Core idea:

Belief comes before skill.

Short script:

```text
B is for Belief.
You do not need to code.
You do not need to be a tech expert.
You do not need to know everything before you begin.
If you can text, you can use AI.
Start with one question.
Then ask the next one.
Belief comes before skill.
```

Visual direction:

A learner hesitates in front of a phone or laptop. Major stands beside them with calm mentor energy. The first simple question is typed. The room warms. Confidence rises.

CTA:

`Comment BELIEF if you are ready to start.`

### Video 5 — C Is For Context

Title:

`AI Does Not Read Minds`

Purpose:

Teach the first practical AI behavior without getting technical.

Core idea:

Better context creates better output.

Short script:

```text
C is for Context.
AI does not read your mind.
It responds to what you give it.
Weak input creates weak output.
Clear context creates better results.
Tell AI who it is for.
Tell it what you need.
Tell it what outcome you want.
Same tool.
Different result.
```

Visual direction:

Split-screen or floating prompt cards. One vague prompt fades. One clear prompt expands into organized output. Avoid heavy text. Use visual contrast: clutter vs clarity.

CTA:

`Comment CONTEXT if you want the better prompt formula.`

### Video 6 — Weekly Session Reminder

Title:

`Bring One Problem`

Purpose:

Drive attendance to the weekly session with a practical invitation.

Core idea:

Do not come trying to learn everything. Come with one real problem.

Short script:

```text
For this week's knowledge session, bring one problem.
Not ten.
One.
Something that wastes your time.
Something you keep avoiding.
Something you want to understand better.
We are going to use the ABCs to look at it clearly.
Awareness.
Belief.
Context.
Then we build from there.
```

Visual direction:

Major at a wooden table or studio desk. A notebook opens. One problem is written down. The letters A, B, C appear as a simple framework around the page.

CTA:

`Bring one problem to the next session.`

## Production Rule

Do not wait for Remotion, Claude Code, YouTube completion, or full app infrastructure before shipping this week's videos.

The weekly knowledge session needs momentum.

Use the current workflow:

1. Generate or reuse cinematic shots.
2. Add voiceover.
3. Add music motif.
4. Add minimal text overlay.
5. End with MAIM seal and tagline.
6. Store raw and edited files in the Vault.
7. Track prompts and final scripts in GitHub.

## 2-Generation Daily Constraint

If using Gemini, Veo, Hailuo, or another limited-generation video platform, follow the established rule:

> 1 Letter = 2 Hero Shots = Unlimited Edits

For weekly knowledge session videos, prioritize:

- One invitation video
- One teaching video

Every shot should be reusable as:

- Instagram Reel
- TikTok
- YouTube Short
- Webinar intro
- B-roll
- Course opening
- Podcast visualizer
- Remotion asset

## Tone Rules

Do not sound like a tech bro.

Do not sell fear.

Do not over-explain.

Do not make AI the hero.

The tone is:

- Calm
- Clear
- Warm
- Grounded
- Human
- Practical
- Culturally rooted
- Beginner-safe

The feeling should be:

> Come sit down. We are going to learn this together.
