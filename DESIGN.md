# Major AI Mindset Design System

This is the canonical design system for MAIM.

No random visuals. No generic AI brand. Everything should feel like one world.

Codex, Claude Code, Google AI Studio, Cowork, and future agents must read this file before making visual decisions.

## Brand Feeling

Caribbean diaspora. New York championship energy. AI sovereignty. Beginner-friendly empowerment. Legacy-building. Human confidence. Spiritual intelligence. Operator clarity.

Premium but not distant. Powerful but not intimidating. Warm but not soft. Cultural but not cliché. Technical but not cold.

## Visual Law

Black foundation. Gold authority. Jamaican green legacy. Knicks blue/orange champion energy. White clarity.

The social media assets invite people to the room.

The landing page is the room.

Do not make the landing page look like the flyer.

## Current Landing Page Correction

The current Codex interpretation is too boxed in.

It repeats the same visual idea in multiple cards.

It uses too many square poster-like blocks.

It feels like a social media flyer stretched into a webpage.

It does not have enough elegance, air, flow, or cinematic movement.

It is too close to the asset style and not close enough to the Google AI Studio landing page feeling.

## Correct Landing Page Design Ratio

Use this ratio for the MAIM landing page:

```txt
65–70% Google AI Studio command-room / elegant universe feel
30–35% MAIM current brand asset language
```

That means the page should inherit MAIM colors, logo, voice, and trust signals.

But the layout, spacing, flow, and atmosphere should come from the Google AI Studio direction.

## Design North Star

The visitor should feel like they are stepping into a universe.

Not clicking an ad.

Not reading a flyer.

Not looking at a social media graphic.

The site should feel like:

- A live knowledge room
- A command room
- A portal into Major's thinking
- A premium system interface
- A calm learning environment
- A schedule and onboarding hub

## Typography

Use typography to create hierarchy, not noise.

Recommended roles:

- Display headlines: Oswald, Archivo Black, Inter ExtraBold, Anton, Bebas Neue, League Spartan
- Emotional editorial lines: Cormorant Garamond or Cinzel for sovereign moments
- Body copy: Inter, Manrope, Montserrat
- Technical labels: JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono, Inter with tracking

Rules:

- Do not use all-caps everywhere.
- Use all-caps for display moments and labels only.
- Use serif for elegance and breath.
- Avoid stacking too many giant block headlines in sequence.
- Let some sections speak quietly.

## Logo System

Primary MAIM logo preference:

Use the circular gold seal logo that combines the `MD` mark with the woven palm hat / crown.

This is the preferred identity mark for MAIM because it carries the founder signature and the crown symbolism in one asset.

Use this circular gold seal for:

- Instagram posts
- Reel covers
- Webinar graphics
- Course visuals
- Watermarks
- Footer marks
- Thumbnail stamps
- Community badges
- Lead magnets
- Landing pages

Avoid using the square green/gold `MD` mark as the primary logo unless a specific template, technical UI, or older asset requires it.

The circular gold hat + MD seal should be treated as the default trust stamp.

## Crown System

The woven palm crown is the signature artifact. Use it as hero object, founder symbol, highlight icon, badge, pin, merchandise concept, course emblem, and community totem.

The crown represents Caribbean roots, ancestral wisdom, humility, authority, memory, legacy, and builder identity.

Use it with reverence.

## Landing Page Layout Rules

The page should flow in scenes, not boxes.

Scene rhythm:

```txt
Open the room
Show the next session
Explain the signal
Reveal the roadmap
Show the live rhythm
Invite the visitor to choose their lane
Introduce Major
Capture the seat
Answer questions
```

Use cards only when they help comprehension.

Do not put every idea inside a square.

Use alternating layouts:

- Full-width hero
- Floating schedule strip
- Three-column signal deck
- Split editorial section
- Timeline or horizontal schedule
- Soft CTA form panel

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

- Big duplicate cards repeating the same idea
- Poster-within-poster layouts
- Too many square boxes
- Heavy bordered cards everywhere
- Loud all-caps blocks stacked without rhythm
- Flyer energy
- Webinar ad energy
- Generic AI course language
- Robotic futuristic imagery
- Overuse of orange
- Repeating the same headline in two nearby sections
- Making the site feel like a carousel slide

## What To Do Instead

Use:

- More negative space
- Softer section transitions
- Fewer cards
- Wider cinematic panels
- Thin lines instead of heavy boxes
- Floating panels instead of boxed posters
- Calm command-room UI
- A visible next-session schedule
- A clear weekly rhythm
- Elegant typography mix
- Subtle grid or orbit background
- One strong idea per section

## Hero Direction

The hero should feel like the AI Studio command-room screenshot.

Hero should include:

- MAIM logo
- Live Knowledge Room label
- Dark grid or orbit environment
- AI Mindset headline
- One supporting statement
- One primary CTA
- One secondary CTA
- A command-view or schedule module

Hero should not include:

- Repeated roadmap card inside the hero
- Large duplicated poster mockups
- Too many competing boxed elements
- Cluttered flyer details

Hero headline:

```txt
AI MINDSET
```

Supporting headline:

```txt
Stop carrying your ideas in your head. Build from where you are.
```

Supporting copy:

```txt
A live learning room for beginners, creators, community leaders, and builders who are ready to stop fearing AI and start using it with confidence.
```

Footer line:

```txt
AI is the tool. You are the future.
```

## Schedule Direction

The landing page must show the next live knowledge session.

This is not optional.

The schedule should feel like a command module, not a flyer block.

Required content:

```txt
Next Knowledge Session
Sunday, [date]
7:00 AM Bali / WITA
Saturday, [date]
7:00 PM New York / ET
Live on Google Meet
```

Weekly rhythm:

```txt
This Week in the Room
A live idea
A working example
A simple action
Replay and follow up
```

## ABC Roadmap Direction

Do not repeat the same visual twice.

If the page shows:

```txt
One Letter. One Lesson. One Action.
```

then do not place a smaller duplicated version next to it.

Use one of these patterns:

Option A:

A single elegant roadmap section with letters A–E.

Option B:

A horizontal roadmap strip.

Option C:

A minimal vertical sequence with lots of air.

Do not use a poster mockup next to the same roadmap content.

## Major Section Direction

Meet Major should feel editorial and human.

Use a real image when available.

Do not overbox the portrait.

Use subtle framing, not a heavy poster card.

Core copy:

```txt
Major Dream Williams helps people translate AI into real life, real business, and real legacy.
```

Anchor line:

```txt
The hero is not AI. The hero is the human learning how to use it.
```

## Register Section Direction

The form should feel like a calm system input.

Not a sales box.

Not an ad block.

Use:

- Full name
- Email
- Current lane
- Register Free
- Backup Google Form

Keep webhook and Google Form fallback.

## Social Asset Rules

Every social asset must pass the 5-second scroll test.

Use one strong headline, one visual metaphor, one CTA, clear logo placement, safe margins, and high contrast.

The image carries clarity. The caption carries depth.

Instagram Feed: 1080 x 1350. One idea per post. Big headline. Strong visual. Minimal copy.

Story/Reel: 1080 x 1920. Hook top, visual center, CTA lower third, logo bottom.

Carousel: one idea per slide. Hook, problem, reframe, lesson, example, action, CTA.

YouTube: 1280 x 720. Three to five words max. Big readable text.

Webinar: clean feed flyer plus detailed distribution flyer.

Ebook: black background, gold title, crown/logo, one strong visual.

Landing page: dark, premium, warm, focused, flowing, command-room feeling. Hero, next session, audience, methodology, live rhythm, founder note, CTA, FAQ.

## Pro Max UI/UX Criteria

Before any visual pass is accepted, answer:

1. Does this feel like a landing page, not a flyer?
2. Does the page flow, or is it trapped in boxes?
3. Is the next knowledge session visible?
4. Is the CTA clear?
5. Does the page feel elegant enough?
6. Does it preserve the AI Studio feel?
7. Does it still feel like MAIM?
8. Is the logo used cleanly?
9. Is orange restrained?
10. Is there duplicated content nearby?
11. Does mobile still feel intentional?
12. Can someone understand what this is within five seconds?

## Final Design Test

Is it clear in five seconds?  
Does it feel like MAIM?  
Does it honor the crown?  
Does it use the preferred circular gold MD + crown seal when a logo is needed?  
Does it help a beginner feel safe?  
Does it feel like a live knowledge room instead of a social media ad?
